Profile of a Trojan Essay

Summary: During freshmen year, I saw myself as a victim and hardly tried on anything. I constantly skipped school and spent my free time playing video games. Sophomore year was fairly similar, and I made it by with Bs and Cs. During Junior year, I decided to take harder classes. I was in Spanish II, AP Lang, Honors Algebra II, Chem 105/106, Honors U.S. History, while working for the tech company AppFire.

I ended up having a terrible skateboard accident, and the ambulance wrecked on the way to the ER. I had 3 brain bleeds, one 4 inches long. I also broke my shoulder and arm, fractured my shin, and ripped my chest off of my shoulder. My parents are both nurses in brain trauma units, and agreed with the surgeon that I had a zero percent chance of survival. I had to say bye to my family. For four days straight I had to be woken up hourly and do multiple tests.

I ended up living. I pushed every day to re-learn how to walk. When I was finally cleared to go home, I had to spend 2 weeks in a pitch black room. I was slowly cleared to see light, move, read, and use technology. Right when I was cleared to read, Senior year started. I spent every day at school trying to get my work done, and every night doing my WKU classes from last year.

But, I pushed through and finished last year with an 89 average. This has taught me the value of hard work and to appreciate what opportunities I am given. I demonstrated accountability in finishing last year's work, with an 89 average, despite the challenges.
